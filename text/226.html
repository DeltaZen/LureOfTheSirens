Cold blooded murder is not really your style, but at the same time, you’ll be forever looking over your shoulder if you let Grimm live.

“Do it.” You reluctantly tell Vanessa, wincing as the blade flashes and Grimm gurgles in a shower of his own blood, twitching for a few moments before lying still.

“Thank you.” You tell Vanessa, as she gets to her feet and steps over Grimm’s corpse. She returns to her usual redheaded incarnation before untying your bonds. “I’m sorry you had to do that. I owe you one.”

“Don’t worry, I won’t forget.” Vanessa tells you grimly. “Now let’s get out of here.”

 

You cross the warehouse away from the door from which Grimm had emerged and carefully open another door. Relieved to find it unguarded, you slip out into the suspended street outside.

“We’re not far from Hector’s hanger.” You tell Vanessa, recognising your location. “We’re on the same level and it’s only ten minutes’ walk from here.” Vanessa nods distractedly, a faraway look in her eyes. “You okay?”

“I don’t know.” She shrugs. “It’s not like we haven’t killed anyone before. They’ve normally got a gun in their hand, that’s all.”

“He was no less dangerous.” You reassure her, though it’s not sitting well with your conscience either. “He’d have chased us across the galaxy in pursuit of his misguided vengeance.”

Vanessa nods and you walk on in silence.

 

You are nearly back to the hanger when you hear a commotion behind you. You turn to see Yasmin and the rest of Grimm’s cohorts chasing after you. They are still a distance behind, but closing fast. Without your blasters, your only option is to try to outpace them.

“Quick, run!” You tell Vanessa, breaking into a sprint, the lights of Hector’s hanger just ahead.
