“I thought you just did that.” You quip. Vanessa rolls her eyes and you gaze at Grimm’s defiant face. Tempting as it would be to leave his corpse behind, you did leave him to take the fall all those years ago.

“No, we’re not murderers.” You tell Vanessa before turning to Grimm. “I didn’t betray you on that job. You got caught, I didn’t. It was as simple as that. I’d say after this little misunderstanding, we’re even. Would you agree?”

“Not like I have a choice.” Grimm replies, Vanessa’s blade still at his throat.

“I thought you’d see it that way.” You grin before your smile vanishes. “But know that if our paths should cross again in the future,” You add darkly, “I won’t be feeling so charitable. Understood?”

“Yeah.” Grimm spits. Vanessa carefully slips the blade from his throat before smashing the handle into the side of his head, knocking him unconscious.

“Thank you.” You tell Vanessa, as she gets to her feet and steps over Grimm’s unconscious form. She returns to her usual redheaded incarnation before untying your bonds. “I owe you one.”

“I’ll add it to your tally.” Vanessa grins. “Now let’s get out of here.”

 

You cross the warehouse away from the door from which Grimm had emerged and carefully open another door. Relieved to find it unguarded, you slip out into the suspended street outside.

“We’re not far from Hector’s hanger.” You tell Vanessa, recognising your location. “We’re on the same level and it’s only ten minutes’ walk from here. This was easier than I thought.”

“Speak for yourself.” Vanessa grumbles, sticking her tongue out and grimacing.

“You didn’t have to finish him off.” You point out.

“I did get a bit carried away.” Vanessa admits with a sheepish smile, blushing.

 

You are nearly back to the hanger when you hear a commotion behind you. You turn to see Yasmin and the rest of Grimm’s cohorts chasing after you. They are still a distance behind, but closing fast. Without your blasters, your only option is to try to outpace them.

“Quick, run!” You tell Vanessa, breaking into a sprint, the lights of Hector’s hanger just ahead.
