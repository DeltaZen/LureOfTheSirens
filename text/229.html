“Leave it to me.” You tell Vanessa confidently. “Yasmin should be back soon. Even tied up, I can best that waif of a girl.”

 

A few minutes later, Yasmin crosses the warehouse towards you, a cup of water in each hand. She reaches Vanessa first, carefully guiding the cup to her lips and tipping it up, Vanessa sipping from it. She then moves across to you, her eyes narrowed warily. She raises the glass to your lips and you make a show of struggling.

“Please, closer.” You ask her hoarsely. She moves closer, just letting her guard down for a moment and you strike, smashing your head into hers. She reels backwards in surprise and you sweep her legs out from under her with yours. She crashes to the ground, her head hitting the ground hard and knocking her unconscious. You feel a pang of guilt at knocking out a woman, but remind yourself that she is a violent mercenary thug. You quickly swing around the column with your back to her, blindly stretching out with your hands to try to grasp her knife.

“Quickly!” Vanessa hisses.

“Not helping!” You shoot back as you stretch out, feeling the knife handle with your fingertips. You work it out of her belt and quickly manipulate it, slicing through the ropes binding your wrists. You get to your feet and cut Vanessa free, the redhead rubbing her wrists and glancing with apprehension at the door, perhaps expecting Grimm to return at any moment. As you hurry across the warehouse in the opposite direction towards another door, you hear Yasmin let out a groggy groan behind you. Relieved to find the door unguarded, you slip out into the suspended street outside.

“We’re not far from Hector’s hanger.” You tell Vanessa, recognising the location. “We’re on the same level and it’s only ten minutes’ walk from here.

“You are a lucky son of a bitch.” Vanessa shakes her head at you, a smile playing at the corners of her mouth.

 

You are nearly back to the hanger when you hear a commotion behind you. You turn to see Grimm and the rest of his cohorts chasing after you. They are still a distance behind, but closing fast. Without your blasters, your only option is to try to outpace them.

“Quick, run!” You tell Vanessa, breaking into a sprint, the lights of Hector’s hanger just ahead.
