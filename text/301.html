You drop out of hyperspace in orbit around Tau Ceti IV, manoeuvring to dock with Omega station. You have never seen the remote station before, but find yourself a little underwhelmed by the basic structure with various habitation modules linked together with struts. You know the station is old, but it looks positively ancient. You manoeuvre alongside and extend the Eclipse’s docking tube to the medical module’s airlock.

 

A few minutes later, the airlock cycles and the door slides open. You step inside the medical module, the air smelling strongly of disinfectant. A glass door slides open as you approach it and you step into a ward, the rows of beds surprisingly unoccupied for a station in the middle of an epidemic.

“Where is everyone?” Vanessa murmurs. “You think we’re too late?”

“Looking pretty deserted.” You agree. “Hello!” You shout. “Anyone here?” You see movement out of the corner of your eye as a figure emerges from a small stock room to your right and rushes towards you.

“Thank God!” A female voice gushes as she throws her arms around you. “I thought we might be stranded here forever!” You prise the woman, a young blonde in a white nurse’s uniform from you and gaze into her tear stained face.

“What’s going on here?” You ask her.

“Everyone’s gone.” A male voice replies from across the room. A man in a doctor’s uniform strides towards you and shakes you warmly by the hand.

“Dead?” Vanessa asks.

“Hopefully not.” He grins. “They’ve all left. It’s just Ffion and myself now.” The doctor is perhaps forty years old, tall with grey flecked dark hair while the nurse, Ffion looks fifteen years his junior. Her white uniform dress hugs her slim figure, her slender legs clad in white stockings and her blonde hair is pinned beneath a white nurse’s hat.

“As medical staff, we were the only ones already immunised against the Xevon virus.” Ffion tells you, wiping the tears from her eyes. “When the outbreak began to spread and people realised that the antidote could take weeks to arrive, they panicked and decided to take their chances crossing the nebula. They all boarded a bulk freighter and left three days ago. Apart from Quentin and I, only the commander remained behind. He died yesterday.”

“Sorry to hear that.” You reply.

“Sorry you’ve risked your lives for nothing.” The doctor, Quentin tells you, before leaning forward and grinning conspiratorially: “Still, if it’s any consolation we’ve got access to the entire station’s alcohol store to ourselves until they return...”

 

A few hours later, all four of you are suitably inebriated. Vanessa sits next to you on one of the hospital beds, while Ffion and Quentin sit on the bed opposite. The two medics have been easy and pleasant company and you have had an enjoyable and relaxing evening after the tensions of the last few days.

“Are you two together?” Vanessa asks, perhaps noticing their overfamiliar body language. Ffion and Quentin glance at each other and laugh.

“Sometimes.” Ffion giggles. “Neither of us want to limit ourselves to a strictly monogamous relationship.”

“Oh.” Vanessa smiles, blushing slightly.

“So,” Quentin grins, gazing at Vanessa, “What about you two? Are you together, exclusive?”

You glance at Vanessa, who shrugs noncommittally.

“Kind of.” You reply warily, though your curiosity is piqued. “Why, what did you have in mind?”

“Do you really need us to spell it out?” Ffion smiles, standing up and beginning to unbutton her white nurse’s uniform, her cleavage slowly and enticingly revealed.
